Format a paper in 3 steps

  1. Add your paper. Upload a .docx, .txt, .md, or .tex file, or paste the text. The first line is read as the title, followed by authors, then Abstract, Keywords:, numbered sections, and References. Leave a blank line between each block for best results.
  2. Pick a publisher. Choose IEEE, Springer (LNCS), or Elsevier. The layout, fonts, headings, and reference style match that publisher's template.
  3. Download. Get a ready-to-edit Word (.docx), a print-ready PDF, or a LaTeX (.tex) source you can compile on Overleaf.

Rewrite AI-sounding text

  1. Paste or load text. On the AI → Human tab, paste your draft, or load a file and pick a single paragraph.
  2. Choose a strength. Light simplifies vocabulary only; Medium also removes filler and splits long sentences; Strong adds contractions for a casual tone. Toggle individual options if you want fine control.
  3. Review & reuse. Click Humanize, read the result, then copy it or download a .txt. Always proofread before submitting.

Tips & notes

  • Everything runs locally in your browser — your files are never uploaded.
  • For a publisher-perfect PDF, download the .tex and compile it free on Overleaf.
  • The Download PDF button opens a print view — choose “Save as PDF” as the destination and allow pop-ups.
  • Reading existing PDF files as input needs the full desktop version on GitHub.
  • The humanizer is a writing aid for your own drafts — not a tool to bypass AI detectors.

About PaperForge

PaperForge is a free, open-source tool that turns plain drafts into properly formatted academic papers for the major publishers, and helps rewrite stiff, AI-sounding prose into clearer language. It runs entirely in your browser — there is no server, no account, and nothing you load ever leaves your device.
